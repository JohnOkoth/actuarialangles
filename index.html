Building a Hybrid Model

Combining traditional Generalized Linear Models (GLMs) with Machine Learning (ML) techniques offers the best of both worlds. Hybrid models leverage the interpretability of GLMs and the predictive power of ML models to enhance pricing accuracy.

  • Key steps in developing a hybrid model:
    • Data preprocessing and feature engineering.
    • Training and validating GLM and ML models independently.
    • Combining outputs using weighting, bias adjustments, or residual modeling.
    • Evaluating the performance with metrics like Gini, Lift Charts, and RMSE.

Importance of External Data

Incorporating external data sources can enrich pricing models, improve segmentation, and enable insurers to identify hidden risks. Common external data sources include:

  • Weather patterns and geographical risk data.
  • Demographic and socioeconomic data.
  • Industry benchmarks and economic indicators.

Price Optimization

Price optimization uses predictive models to adjust premiums based on customer behavior, risk profiles, and competitive data. Key aspects include:

  • Understanding price elasticity of demand.
  • Balancing profitability with market competitiveness.
  • Simulating scenarios to assess impacts on retention and new business.

Learn About Price Optimization

Integrating Usage-Based Insurance (UBI)

Usage-Based Insurance leverages telematics data to personalize premiums based on driving behavior. Integration into pricing models involves:

  • Capturing data on braking, acceleration, cornering, and mileage.
  • Calculating scores for driving behavior and assigning weights to key metrics.
  • Adjusting base premiums based on these scores.
